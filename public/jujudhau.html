Jujudhau Recipe

Ingredients:

  1. 1 liter full-fat milk
  2. 2 tablespoons plain yogurt (for starter culture)
  3. 1/2 cup sugar (optional)
  4. 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder (optional)

Procedures:

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pot, bring the milk to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.
  2. Once the milk boils, reduce the heat and let it simmer until it reduces to about 3/4 of its original volume. Stir frequently.
  3. Optionally, add sugar and cardamom powder to the milk and mix well.
  4. Let the milk cool down to a lukewarm temperature (about 110°F or 43°C).
  5. In a small bowl, mix the plain yogurt with a few tablespoons of the cooled milk to create a smooth mixture.
  6. Stir the yogurt mixture back into the cooled milk.
  7. Pour the mixture into a clay pot or any other container you prefer.
  8. Cover the container with a lid or cloth and let it sit undisturbed in a warm place for 6-8 hours or overnight, until the yogurt is set and has a thick, creamy texture.
  9. Once set, refrigerate the Juju Dhau for a few hours before serving.
  10. Serve chilled and enjoy your delicious Juju Dhau!
