Thenthuk Recipe

Ingredients:

  1. 200g all-purpose flour
  2. 1/2 cup water
  3. 1 onion, thinly sliced
  4. 2 tomatoes, chopped
  5. 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  6. 1/2 cup cabbage, shredded
  7. 1/2 cup carrots, thinly sliced
  8. 1/2 cup spinach, chopped
  9. 1/2 cup beans, chopped
  10. 1/2 cup mushrooms, sliced
  11. 1/2 cup cooked chicken or beef, shredded (optional)
  12. 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  13. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  14. 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  15. 1 teaspoon chili powder
  16. 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  17. Salt to taste
  18. Oil for cooking
  19. Fresh cilantro for garnish

Procedures:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the all-purpose flour with water to form a stiff dough. Knead the dough for about 5-10 minutes until smooth. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Roll out the dough thinly on a floured surface. Cut the dough into thin strips to make the noodles. Set aside.
  3. Heat oil in a large pot. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add sliced onions, chopped tomatoes, and green chilies. Sauté until the tomatoes are soft.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, chili powder, and soy sauce. Mix well.
  6. Add the sliced vegetables and mushrooms. Sauté for a few minutes.
  7. Add water or chicken/beef broth to the pot and bring to a boil.
  8. Add the hand-pulled noodles to the boiling broth. Cook for about 5-7 minutes or until the noodles are cooked through.
  9. Season with salt and add cooked chicken or beef if using.
  10. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro. Enjoy your comforting bowl of Thenthuk!
