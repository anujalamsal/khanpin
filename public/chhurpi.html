Chhurpi Recipe

Ingredients:

  1. Yak Milk or Cow Milk
  2. Lime or Lemon Juice
  3. Himalayan Salt
  4. Liquid Smoke (optional)

Procedures:

  1. Heat the milk to 90 degree Celsius to kill microbes and harmful bacteria.
  2. Separate the fat from the milk through the Centrifugation Process.
  3. Add a small quantity of lime juice and Himalayan salt to the separated milk and heat it to form semi-solid cheese through the coagulation process.
  4. Pack the coagulated cheese into rectangular blocks, wrap it in a jute cloth, and compress it with high force to extract and remove excess water.
  5. Cut the dehydrated blocks into long bars of hard Churpi.
  6. Smoke dry the Churpi bars for 28 days to add a natural preservative layer.
